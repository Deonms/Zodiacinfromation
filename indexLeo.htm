what to do when a Leo is mad?:

When a Leo is angry, they can be dramatic and passionate in their reactions, so it’s important to handle their emotions carefully. Here’s what you can do:
1.Give Them Attention: Leos love to feel seen and appreciated. When they’re angry, make sure you give them your full attention and let them express how they feel. Listen to them without interrupting.
2.Avoid Ignoring Them: Leos can feel hurt if you ignore them or don't acknowledge their feelings. Instead, offer them understanding and empathy. Let them know you care and that their feelings matter.
3.Apologize Sincerely: If you've done something to upset them, a genuine, heartfelt apology can go a long way. Leos appreciate sincerity and won’t respond well to insincere or half-hearted apologies.
4.Validate Their Ego: Leos are ruled by the Sun and have a strong sense of pride. Reassure them that you respect them and their worth. Compliment their strengths or express gratitude for what they bring to the relationship. Just be careful not to overdo it or sound patronizing.
5.Give Them Space to Cool Off: Like anyone, Leos may need some time to calm down after a heated moment. Allow them to step back and reflect without pushing for an immediate resolution.
6.Don’t Challenge Them Publicly: If the issue arose in a social setting, don’t try to correct or challenge them in front of others. Leos value their pride and dignity, so addressing the situation privately will likely make them feel respected.
Once they feel heard, respected, and valued, Leos usually cool down quickly. Just give them the space and acknowledgment they need to feel understood.
Leos typically stay angry for a few hours to a day, depending on the situation. They are passionate and expressive, so their anger may flare up quickly but usually dissipates just as fast once they feel acknowledged and respected. However, if their pride has been significantly wounded or they feel disrespected, their anger could linger for a few days. The key to resolving Leo’s anger is offering a sincere apology and showing them that their feelings and pride are important.
